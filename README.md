# Sales Analysis
### Project Overview
---
This data analysis project aims to provide insights into the sales perfomance of an e-commerce company over the past year. By analyzing various aspects of the sales data, we seek to identify trends, make data driven recommendations, and gain a deeper understanding of the company's perfomance.

### Data sources
---
Sales Data: The primary dataset used for this analysis "sales_data.csv" file, containing detailed information about each sale made by the company.

### Tools

### Exploratory Data Analysis
---
EDA involved exploring the sales data to answer key questions, such as:
1. How are sales and profits changing over months, quarters, and years?
2. Which customer segments bring in the most revenue and profit?
3. What products are best sellers and most profitable?
4. How do sales and profits vary by country?
5. Are discounts impacting profitability significantly?
6. Where should the company focus sales and marketing efforts to improve returns?

## Recommendations
---
Based on the analysis, we recommend the following actions:
1. Prioritize the "Summer Spike" (April - August)
- Action: Concentrate at least 60% of your annual marketing budget into this 5-month window.
2. Double Down on the Government Segment
- Action: Create a dedicated B2G (Business-to-Government) sales team. Offer them exclusive volume-based pricing, longer payment terms, or dedicated account managers.
3. Use "Paseo" as Your Lead Magnet
(Paseo is your best-selling and most profitable product.)
- Action: Bundle Paseo with your lower-performing products. Offer a "Buy Paseo, get 20% off Product X" deal. Use Paseo in your marketing ads to draw customers in, but upsell them on complementary items.
4. Eliminate "High Discounts" entirely. Cap all discounts at a maximum of 15%.
5. Introduce "Micro-Loyalty" discounts: Instead of giving 30% off to everyone, give 5% off to everyone, but give an extra 10% off only to customers who buy a minimum of 3 units. This increases volume without destroying your per-unit profit.
6. For the "Zero Discount" months (Sept-Dec): Your sales plummeted to $6.8M. You must offer a small discount (5-10%) during these months, but only on bundles (e.g., "Buy 2 Paseos, get 10% off"). Never offer a straight discount on a single item again.
